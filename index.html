Student Leaders (SLs)

        are student-gamers from different universities that have undergone a strict selection process to organize and facilitate campus-wide events and make the Mobile Legends Bang Bang Community known on their campus. SLs, once accepted, will do tasks related to MLBB such as organizing and facilitating online tournaments, activities, and contests with the goal of motivating the student MLBB players to pursue playing the game while still pursuing academics. In line with all of this, SLs also have the responsibility to help Moonton Employees with conducting surveys and further studies on the campus in hopes of the betterment of the said mobile game. Student Leaders are being continuously trained to become and stay a well-respected, friendly student-gamer with integrity that aims to build a community on their campus where all is safe and secure. There is nothing an SL can’t do.

What are MLBB Student Leaders?

        MLBB Student Leaders (SL) are well-selected students who are averagely and usually good at their academics. They will be trained to have capabilities in organizing campus-wide MLBB online events as well as passively being the MLBB community leaders in their school. SLs should be well-respected friendly students with integrity.

About Us

         On September 2020, Moonton Philippines has started out the establishment of MSL Philippines, or widely known as MLBB PH – Student Leaders. As the COVID-19 Pandemic continues to hit the Philippines, student-gamers have stayed at home. To further promote healthy and balanced gaming without affecting academics, MSL PH has interviewed applicants interested to become part of the starting family. MSL Philippines has been established officially in October of 2020, with its first batch of interviewed and accepted in Student Leaders. SLs since then organized online MLBB tournament, activities, and contests successfully and continues to do so as of the moment. The family has been continuously growing and stays true to their words: Balanced Gaming and Academics. As of date, the MSL Communities are composed of 72 College Schools around the Philippines: 34 in Luzon, 15 in Visayas, and 23 in Mindanao.
